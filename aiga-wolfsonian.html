Design from 1914

August 23rd, 2014

For years, I’d heard good things about AIGA (American Institute of Graphic Arts, for the uninitiated). Before moving to South FL, just didn’t have the opportunity to get involved in a local chapter, since my city didn’t have one, and the closest chapters were 3+ hours away.

So imagine my excitement when I found out that not only does Miami have an AIGA chapter, but that it was conducting a 90-day call for volunteers to get new people involved! AIGA-national is celebrating its centennial – the association was founded in 1914 – and local chapters are organizing events that help commemorate its history. AIGA Miami’s 90-day Volunteer program is focused on creating events and design related to the national celebration.

I was asked to design promotional materials for a centennial celebration event in conjunction with the Wolfsonian–FIU museum. AIGA Miami planned a “Salon” style evening at the Wolfsonian, where designers could have a private viewing of posters, books, and prints from the Wolfsonian archives – all from the year 1914! I love how design interacts with culture, politics, art, philosopy, and world events, so designing for this historical project was a lot of fun for me.

Here’s the art I produced, which used a print from the Wolfsonian’s archives, along with the “100 YEARS OF DESIGN” logo from AIGA national. This design was used across a variety of channels in promoting the event.

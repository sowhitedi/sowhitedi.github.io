100 Days of Loteria

March 27, 2015

The Great Discontent is teaming up with designer-turned-artist Elle Luna to run The 100 Day Project, a celebration of process that encourages everyone to participate in 100 days of making. I decided to take up the challenge with a little idea that’s been rolling around in my head for awhile.

Mine will be “100 Days of Loteria” – drawing on the rich and quirky iconography of the traditional Mexican game Loteria, I’ll create & publish an icon-like illustration inspired by the cards, and a brief story of the card to accompany the illustration. I’ll be posting to my Instagram account (@sowhitedi) and using the hashtag #100DaysOfLoteria.

Loteria is a bit similar to bingo, where players have boards and a caller announces what to match – though rather than matching up numbers, players find & match pictures. The pictures are an odd assortment of animals, characters, objects, and symbols, with a bit of a tarot-card feeling.

But the real fun of Loteria is that the caller isn’t simply announcing the cards – “The Deer,” “The Mandolin,” “The Palm Tree” – she tells a story, an anecdote, a rhyme, or an inside joke related to the card. The game takes on a rapid, hilarious cadence and each caller has her own style.

Loteria decks have 54 cards. There are many different makers of Loteria and as a result, variations and alternate cards. Although the sets include many of the same cards, there’s no strict canon for Loteria cards and there are different illustration styles for the different editions.

For my 100 Days of Loteria, I will use the most common cards as a starting point for my sequence. In order to create a consistent set of icons, I plan to use a juicy black mono line style, which is a style I haven’t used much and want to really explore. I’ll be using the original Spanish titles for the cards but providing a translation to English.

I’ll also create a personal description of the card – whatever my association is for the card. This part will be quite interesting as some of the cards are more or less mundane (The Boot) and others seem intended as symbols (The Hand).

I'm both excited and nervous about this challenge – making your work and thoughts public daily can be intensely uncomfortable for anyone (esp. for introverts!) but I think this will be a lot of fun.

Anybody want to join me in the #The100DaysProject? Check out this story on The Great Discontent, and let me know what your #100Daysof______ will be!
